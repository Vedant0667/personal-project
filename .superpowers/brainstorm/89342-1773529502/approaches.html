Choose an approach

Three ways to add the missing sections. Pick the one that feels right.

A

Minimal additions

Add CTAs to the existing hero (two buttons below tagline), drop a Skills strip after Achievements, and add a slim contact footer with email + social icons. No restructuring — just plug the gaps.

Pro: Least disruption, fastest to ship
Con: Contact section feels like an afterthought
B

Purposeful additions (recommended)

Update hero with CTAs + GitHub/LinkedIn icons. Add a dedicated Skills section (icon grid, no % bars) after Projects. Add a proper Contact section before a clean footer — email mailto link, social buttons, a short CTA line. Add filter tabs to the Projects section header.

Pro: Each addition feels intentional, matches current polish level
Con: More work than A
C

Full redesign of additions

Everything in B, plus: a full-screen Contact section with a glassmorphism card, an animated skills section with hover reveals, a floating nav that highlights current section, and a sticky "contact me" button. More ambitious, more cohesive.

Pro: Most impressive result
Con: Significantly more implementation, higher risk of breaking existing sections
My recommendation: B. It adds everything important at the quality level your current site already has — without bloating it or risking breaking the scroll/animation interactions you've built.
